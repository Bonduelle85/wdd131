History

Defense of the Ancients (DotA) began its history as a user-created map for the game Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos and its expansion The Frozen Throne. This map was created by fans and quickly gained popularity due to its unique gameplay and strategic approach. In 2009, Valve decided to develop their own version of the game called Dota 2. After several years of development and testing, Dota 2 was released in 2013 and immediately captured the hearts of millions of players worldwide. Today, Dota 2 remains one of the most popular and recognized games in the MOBA genre.

Rules

Dota 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where two teams of five players each compete to destroy the enemy team's Ancient while defending their own. Players select from a roster of heroes with unique abilities and roles. The game is played on a map with three lanes, each guarded by defensive towers. Players earn gold and experience by defeating enemy units and structures to level up and purchase items to strengthen their heroes. Communication and teamwork are essential to success, as players must coordinate strategies, control map objectives, and outmaneuver their opponents to achieve victory. The team that destroys the enemy Ancient first wins the game.
